Prototype Phase

Explore the prototyping phase of LitterLogger, our solution.

About Our Prototype

Through integrative thinking, we combined the pros from some of the ideas we came up with to create a new solution that is both practical and impactful. We chose to focus on a mobile app because of our strength and familiarity in app development. Thus we combined the litter identifier & logger app idea made for volunteers of FORK and the public-facing reward-based mobile app idea.

Our solution is called, LitterLogger, which allows users to report litter, gather data and track clean-up efforts, and encourage users to volunteer with FORK.

Itâ€™s designed to be easy to use for both volunteers and the public, while collecting valuable data for FORK.

Key Features

Geo-tagged Reports

Pinpoint locations with high amount of litter reported using real-time heatmap.

AI image recognition

Capture photos of litter sightings and use AI to easily identify litter type and weight, then upload the photo and report the sighting.

Data Insights

Track trends in littering patterns and gather data on litter picked up such as the litter type and weight.

Protoyping Journey

Flow Diagram

We drew a diagram to help us determine the flow of actions taken by a user. This helps us to better understand the flow of the app and identify the different screens needed.

Lo-Fi Paper Prototype

Then we drew a lo-fi prototype using paper and marker, which was quick and easy as we had the freedom to draw whatever we wanted to include in the app. However, we found it challenging to make our ideas understandable as we were limited by our drawing skills, which was a little disheartening.

Lo-Fi Draw.io Prototype

We moved to using draw.io, due to the convenience of drawing clean shapes and legible words to convey our ideas. Drawing it out helped us see what works and what does not, and we were able to easily change things around to make the UI more intuitive and clearer to use.

Hi-Fi Figma Prototype

Then we translated our lo-fi prototype to Figma for higher fidelity and interactivity. This tests the prototype's usability, to test how intuitive the UI is, and whether the app is easy to use or unnecessarily complex. After drawing what was planned, we tested it out and realized missing features and screens needed to better showcase the app functions. So, we added what was missing and tested it again. This iterative process is what ultimately helped to fine-tune our prototype.

Key Reflections

Convergence

We found it challenging to decide on which idea is the best solution, so through integrative thinking we created a new solution by combining the pros of both the reward-based and litter identifier app ideas.

Teamwork

And as we actively worked on the prototype together, we ran into logical problems with the application, but after a brief discussion, we managed to quickly find the solutions. Hence, it reminds us that working as a team was important in validating our ideas.

Perspective

Prototyping also helped us see from the user perspective more, making us rethink our UI design so that it would be more accessible. Hence, we made the buttons bigger with larger text and accompanying icons.

Testing

And when we tested the prototype, there were issues with the complexity so we decided to make it simpler and reduce the features such as rewarding the reporting of litter, and integrating with FORK's social media page to post photos of litter reported.
