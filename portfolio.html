Week 1: Ideation & Planning

This week marked the official start of our project. We began by speaking with our stakeholder Sally, chairperson of Friends of the River Kelvin (FORK), to better understand the littering and pollution issues affecting the river. Using the Double Diamond design process, we entered the "Discover" phase by asking “How might we?” questions to explore the broader problem and identify user needs, particularly the challenges volunteers face in logging litter data while cleaning.

These insights led us into the "Define" and "Develop" phases, where we held a brainstorming session to explore possible solutions. Sally’s input was crucial here, as she emphasized how difficult it is for volunteers to manually record the type, quantity, and location of litter.

To support collaboration, we finalized our team contract, defined individual roles, and set up a GitHub repository and shared drive to keep our work organized and avoid miscommunication. We ended the week with a clear, user-driven direction and a strong plan to guide our project forward.


Week 2: Prototyping & Testing

During our initial brainstorming and research phase, we looked into existing applications that tackle litter tracking and environmental monitoring. One key suggestion from our stakeholder Sally was the what3words app, that provides a fast and convenient way to provide location to others and was also mentioned to be quite popular recently.

This sparked ideas around how precise location tracking could enhance the effectiveness of litter logging. We also examined apps like Litterati and Marine Debris Tracker, which allow users to geotag litter and categorize it by type. These platforms highlighted the potential of combining geographic data with community engagement to build meaningful datasets. Inspired by their strengths and being mindful of their limitations in data consistency and user participation, we used integrative thinking to weigh both manual and automated models for monitoring litter.

Afterwhich, we developed the flow diagram to better help us visualize the user journey and identify key features for our prototype. We then created a Figma prototype to illustrate how our app, LitterLogger, would allow users to report litter, track clean-up efforts, and support environmental awareness around the River Kelvin.
