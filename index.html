Week 1: Ideation & Planning

This week marked the official start of our project. We began by speaking with our stakeholder Sally, chairperson of Friends of the River Kelvin (FORK), to better understand the littering and pollution issues affecting the river. Using the Double Diamond design process, we entered the "Discover" phase by asking “How might we?” questions to explore the broader problem and identify user needs, particularly the challenges volunteers face in logging litter data while cleaning.

These insights led us into the "Define" and "Develop" phases, where we held a brainstorming session to explore possible solutions. After evaluating our ideas, we decided on creating an AI-powered mobile app to streamline real-time litter logging. Sally’s input was crucial here, as she emphasized how difficult it is for volunteers to manually record the type, quantity, and location of litter. This shaped our app concept, which focuses on automated image recognition and easy data collection.

To support collaboration, we finalized our team contract, defined individual roles, and set up a GitHub repository and shared drive to keep our work organized and avoid miscommunication. We ended the week with a clear, user-driven direction and a strong plan to guide our project forward.

Week 2: Prototyping & Testing

In Week 2, we jumped into development mode by building a basic front-end interface and integrating TensorFlow.js to classify litter images. The first version of our prototype allowed users to upload an image and receive a predicted trash type.

We tested the model in-browser and observed performance variations across devices. This sparked conversations around model optimization and image preprocessing. We also conducted initial usability testing with peers, gathering valuable feedback on the UI/UX.

Iterating quickly, we redesigned the image upload flow and added more descriptive error handling. The prototype began to take shape, and team morale remained high.
